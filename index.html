Feelings after getting the award

Today, finally, I got the silver award for the 2019 BPho.

Looking at the certificate, I had many words to say. Although it is only a silver award, it is quite unique for me. I have known this competition since the summer holiday in 2019. Having a first look at the past paper of BPho, I nearly can’t do any of those questions, because I only knew a little from my IGCSE course, which was absolutely not enough. Then I decided to try my best to work out as many questions as possible. It was really a tough time for me, especially at the beginning. I went to school at daytime, so the only time for me was the evening. Every day, I felt so exhausted after a busy school day, but for the BPho, for my dream, I kept telling myself ‘Come on !’. A few weeks later, I managed to do some of the questions, and I felt a little proud for myself.

On 16th of November, 2019, I attended the BPho. After getting the paper, I focused on my paper and did not think about anything else. As time passed by, the questions becomed more difficult, and at some instant, I was a little bit impatient, because there were more and more questions that I can’t manage to do, but later on, I told myself ‘Calm down! Try your best, and it will be fine’. Therefore, one question successfully reminded me of a question about the spring oscillator, which I have done before, which inspired me a lot. I still remembered that question, which was about letting a double planks connected by two strings falling from a height, and the two strings will make the planks to do simple harmonic motion. This gave me a lot of confidence, and encouraged me to do more questions. Although it was difficult to pay 100% attention throughout the 160 minutes, and there were some questions I did wrong which I should have done it correctly, I was appreciated when the competition has finished, because I applied at least 70% of what I have learnt. Meanwhile, I felt a little bit upset, because I thought there would be some questions about the electric circuits in section 2, such as the Kirchhoff’s law and the Wheatstone bridge, but they did not appear in the paper.

Now, I got the result. I was fortunate, and I want to say thank you to my father, who helped me a lot during the preparation of the BPho, and of course, I want to say thank you to myself. It is me who kept challenging the extreme and never gave up.

This silver award is just a starting point. There is still a long way to go. Well done and keep it up!

The feelings after attending the BPho

On November 15th, I attended the BPho Physics competition. Although the content of the test was a little bit hard, I learned so much from the exam itself and the process of preparing for it.

At the beginning, facing so many difficult questions, I didn’t know what to do. As I was an AS level student, some of the questions are the ones that I hadn’t met before. As a result, after solving some questions, I decided to separate all the questions from 2008 to 2018 into 5 categories in Section 1 and 8 categories in Section 2.


Also, I made some comments, which is about some of my thinking process, on the side of the question.

After taking part in this exam, I have learned 3 good habits:

  • Being persistent. During the learning, not everything can be achieved without any obstacles. Although, once I wanted to give up, I kept telling myself ‘try again, try again’. Luckily, I did it, and regardless of the outcome, this spirit inspired me a lot.

  • Being confident. At the beginning, I was not confident at all, because I can solve few problems. As time went by, I began to make some progress, so I became more confident. Latter, I found that the more confident you are, the better the outcome will be, vice versa.

  • Being ambitious. Before doing a task, a target should be built up, which guided me when I was hesitated. Setting up a higher goal gives me many incentives to learn.

    What is more, after doing a lot of questions, I found that Physics was quite interesting. Many things in our daily lives can be explained by Physics. For example, one day, on my way to school, a car passed by with its trumpet on. The pitch of which changed as the car moving. A week later, I knew this is called the Doppler Effect.

Physics is very interesting!
