How to control the motion of the smart car?

Posted on 2020-08-20 | In Dialogue with Plants |

Yesterday, I have received a smart car that is designed by other people, and decided to learn some principles behind controlling the car.

Schematic diagram

  • On the right, this is Arduino.
  • On the left, this is a driver circuit.
  • A driver circuit is used because it can deliver higher power to drive the motor. Arduino can only deliver relatively low power, say 5V in this case.

Duty cycle

The term duty cycle describes the proportion of ‘on’ time to the regular interval or ‘period’ of time

  • Low duty cycle –> low power
  • High duty cycle –> high power

PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)

Pulse Width Modulation, or PWM, is a technique for getting analogue results (Voltage) with digital means (Square wave).

  • PWM can be used to alter the duty cycle in order to alter the average voltage.
  • According to $P=VI$, the average power delivered to the motor can change as different PWM wave is applied.
  • Therefore, the rotation rate of the motor, i.e. the speed of the car can be controlled.

Programming codes

I have also written some codes to control the car. Once a button is pressed, the car will do the corresponding reaction.

  • A,B are used to control a wheel, and C,D are used to control another wheel.
  • The potential difference between A and B corresponds to the power given to one wheel, and that between C and D corresponds to the power given to the other wheel.
  • In the setup, button1,2,3,4 are pulled up, so that initially, the state of the button is at High level. When the button is pressed, the state changes to Low level.
  • Button1: move at the fastest speed for 5 seconds
  • Button2: move at the fastest speed in the opposite direction for 5 seconds
  • Button3: turn around in a fixed position. The driving forces of two wheels are the same in magnitude but in opposite directions, so they produce a Torque that gives arise to the turning effect.
  • Button4: move at a slower speed in the same direction as Button1 does.
