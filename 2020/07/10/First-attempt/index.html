First attempt

Posted on 2020-07-10 | In Dialogue with Plants |

Yesterday, I have tried to use the Raspberry Pi and Arduino to capture image and humidity data from the plants.

I have put one plant in a dark room with 2 lights shining on it, and it is shown above. The surroundings seems to be a little bit messy, but this is the most suitable place that I found in the Lab where almost no people will pass by.

These are the devices being tested.

During the testing, in order to increase the reliability of the humidity data, I used 2 humidity sensor inserting into the soil, and the two Arduinos connecting these 2 sensors have Ports COM5 and COM6.

These 2 sensors show the same trend when humidity changes, and this is what I expected.

However, I realised that the humdity data fluctuates a lot with the sensor in the same fixed position in the soil, and the range of which can reach 30-40 units. The humidity sensor cannot tell you the exact absolute value of the humidity at that point. It can only tell you the relative change of humidity at that point. Even though, it can tell be relative change, the system is not very stable, because it can be affacted by so many factors. For example, I shaked the flowerport slighly, the fluctuation of the humidity data increases, but in fact, the humidity did not change at all. From the unstable humdity data, I have concluded the following ideas:

  1. Digital or Analogue values are not always stable and reliable, because they can be affected by too many factors, and in other words, you cannot ensure that there are only one variable in reality, meaning that more than one condition are varied and you may not even notice them, but they will affect the normal performance of the electronic devices

  2. Sometimes, visualisation is more reliable than analogue or digital data. Take the plants as an example, if you have some common sense that in what style or shape does the leave show that it is lack of water, you can tell immediately whether the plant needs to be watered at a first glance. Instead of analysing the humidity data which sometimes may be not accurate, visualisation is a quicker and more accurate way to tell the humidity. For example, if you see the leaves turn brown and it seems to be dried out, definitely it needs water.

  3. Here comes the question: we can tell whether the plant needs water as a human being, why do I need to use Machine learning to tell this simple fact? I thought about this question for a while, and comes up with a nice answer: Imagine that there are 100 hectares of plants, is it possible for a human being to check each plant and water them? The answer is NO, and this is why electronic circuits can be helpful to release the burden of human beings. A automatic machine can tell whether this plant needs water by taking an image of the plant and check the image by a machine learning model that gives an YES/NO answer for whether it needs water. If the plant needs water, the water can automatically fall onto it without human labour to do this physically demanding job. This is what the EE can bring convenience and benefits to our daily lives.

  4. Additionally, there are some people who wants to have a plant in their home, but they often forget to water it or do not know when does the plant needs to be watered. In the current market, there are mainly two types of automatic watering system:

    • Timer. Water the plant at a given interval.
    • Humidity sensor. Determine whether to water the plant according to the data read from the humidity sensor.

    However, I think these 2 systems both have their disadvantages. The timer system can only water the plant in a given interval, but in different whether conditions, in different seasons, plants have different demand for the water. If it is only determined by the given interval of time, sometimes it may be misunderstanding of the plants. For the humidity sensor system, like I said before, the humidity data read from the soil can fluctuate to a very large extent, and also, people with no expertise do not know how to adjust the sensor. These will all affect the eventual judgement. Therefore, I came up with the idea of making the decision by visualisatio of the leaves or soil, and I think it will be a more effective way to deal with watering stuffs.
