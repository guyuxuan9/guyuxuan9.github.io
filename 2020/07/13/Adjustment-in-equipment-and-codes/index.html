Last few days, I found that the GUI will exit automatically after 1-2 hours, and the humidity data fluctuates too much, which leads to too many uncertainties. Therefore, I decided to fix these two bugs.

Capacitive Humidity Sensor

I bought a new humidity sensor that determines the humidity by measuring the capacitance of the material. The previous humidity sensor determines the humidity by measuring the resistance of the material. These two types of sensors have some differences in how do they work.

Capacitive :

  • $$C=\dfrac {\varepsilon A}{4\pi kd}$$ , where $\varepsilon$ is the permittivity.
  • When the humidity of the environment changes, the permittivity $\varepsilon$ will change, so the capacitance of the material will change. We can detect this change using the electric circuits embedded inside the board.
  • The permittivity varies almost linearly with the humidity change, so the capacitance varies almost linearly with the humidity change.

Resistive:

  • From the A level Physics coursebook, I also know that:

    $$R=\rho \dfrac {L}{A}$$ , where $\rho$ is the resistivity of the resistor, L is the length of the resistor, and A is the cross-sectional area of the resistor.

  • When humidity of the environment changes, the resistivity $$\rho$$ will change, so the resistance of the material will change, so as the voltage across the material. We can convert the analogue voltage signal to digital signal using ADC.

  • However, the resistive sensor lacks of linearity.

This is the humidity data read from the new capacitive humidity sensor. As shown in the graph above, the fluctuation is very small, so the precision increases.

In this capacitive humidity sensor, the capacitance is converted into voltage signal by electric circuits sealed in the module, so currently, I do not know clearly how does the module achieve this. The analogue voltage signal is read from the capacitive humidity sensor, and then the signal is transmitted to Arduino. The chips in Arduino will convert this analogue signal to digital signal by ADC which is 10 bits, so the value shown on the screen is the digital signal varied from 0-1023. In addition, Arduino will transmit the data to the Raspberry Pi via UART (Universal Asynchronous Receiver-Transmitter), which usually deals with digital signals.
