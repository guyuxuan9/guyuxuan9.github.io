Feelings and Reflections on the Garbage Classification Programme

Being a good student requires a variety of skills including time management, teamwork, keeping good focus and facing challenges, which I have learnt during my experience of taking part in the Garbage Classification Programme which aims to determine which class the rubbish belongs to through image identification and CNN algorithm.

<Image from https://youth-time.eu/wp-content/uploads/2018/11/b7d41aa04e4da12ee42f355b9c8e1b10_XL.jpg>

At the beginning, I had absolutely no idea what the Garbage Classification was and how can it benefit people all over the world. During the process of learning, I gradually found the importance of Garbage Classification which can benefit our economy, society and ecosystem.

< Image from https://ichef.bbci.co.uk/news/1024/cpsprodpb/1BFF/production/_109676170_gettyimages-1166817364-2.jpg >

First of all, with increasing amount of rubbish produced every day and deteriorating environment, people have increasing concern about how to deal with a significant amount of rubbish and the concept of garbage classification was presented. With classified rubbish, the soil erosion will be greatly reduced, because some of the rubbish, such as battery and plastic bags, are not biodegradable, meaning that they cannot or it takes very long time for them to be broken down by the soil. Staying in the soil, those rubbish will make the field useless and decrease the output for the agriculture goods, which will negatively affect the economy.

<Image from https://res.cloudinary.com/fleetnation/image/private/c_fit,w_1120/g_south,l_text:style_gothic2:%C2%A9%20Ashley%20Cooper,o_20,y_10/g_center,l_watermark4,o_25,y_50/v1495745317/sfjlaup4gwdorkymjnqz.jpg>

In addition, some rubbish, such as rot food, will emit gases with bad smell and attracts many flies, which makes citizens very uncomfortable when passing through the region. This has worsened the environment significantly. What is more, some animals will accidently take in some the toxic plastic and die in a few months, which makes the ecosystem unbalanced.

Therefore, the garbage classification is needed to improve the situation. Different classes of rubbish can be reused in different ways, so that the resources can be used more efficiently. For example, disposable chopsticks, which are widely used in China, can be reprocessed to produce paper which will be otherwise produced by cutting down thousands and millions of trees. Also, some rubbish, such as wooden stuff, can be used to burn to produce electricity and providing heat for city dwellers in winter. As a result, different classes of rubbish can be sent to different factories to reprocess them and then reuse them. Therefore, instead of just landfill unclassified rubbish, we can reuse 60%-70% of the rubbish according to their classes to change them from rubbish to resources, and in the battle between human beings and rubbish, the rubbish can be changed from enemies to friends.

<Image from https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0773/5915/articles/Ryclying_pic_blog_2_1600x.jpg?v=1455084942>

However, in China, a large proportion of people are only encouraged to do the garbage classification, but they do not know how. Many people cannot tell which class the rubbish belongs to, so although there are different rubbish bins, they cannot correctly throw the rubbish into the correct bin. Therefore, I decided to learn some AI techniques to give information to people about the classes of the rubbish and help them classify the rubbish more easily. Although some people might argue that it is impossible for the machine to improve the garbage classification, because different classes of rubbish was put together into the rubbish bag by each households and it is very hard for the machine to identify the rubbish in the bag and then classify them, I holds a different view. In some occasions, such as in the airport, people often throw only one or two rubbish into the rubbish bin instead of a bag of them. The AI machine can be used in these situations, telling people the class in which the rubbish should be thrown, so that people get more knowledge about the classes, and when they repeat this in many situations for many times, they will get used to this way of thinking. Therefore, they can correctly classify the rubbish at home.

<Image from https://i.ytimg.com/vi/7C6I_wuvvhY/maxresdefault.jpg>

In reality, in Shanghai, China, it is compulsory for local citizens to do garbage classification, and some people, especially the old people, have little idea of the classes, and this has caused some trouble for their normal daily life. Citizens need to spend a lot of time searching for the type of the rubbish, which is very inefficient and boring. This may lead to some complains among the society, and give disincentives for citizens to do the meaningful things. Therefore, I wonder whether this complex thing can be done easily and interestingly, so I came up with the idea of connecting the garbage classification with modern technology instead of just a rule. The CNN algorithm of machine learning can train a model for a machine which can be used to identify the rubbish quickly and accurately. For instance, citizens only needs to show the rubbish in front of the camera, and then the class will be shown on the displayed screen, so that it will arouse the curiosity of children, adults, mid-aged people and aged-people, so that people can do the meaningful things happily. Due to the fact that it is not necessary for each one to know exact class of the rubbish, the garbage classification campaign can be done more effectively and burden of memory on residents can be reduced.

<Image from https://encrypted-tbn0.gstatic.com/images?q=tbn:ANd9GcS9dWakreHNMCnKp2e05FKeJJhqJ4IiGyz_eJr0YDQPSG8LLZyT&s>

In conclusion, after doing the programme, I have spent some valuable time thinking about the future prospects of the garbage classification that can be applied to our real life, and currently I am still training the model and improve the algorithms. Hopefully, one day in the future, the AI techniques of machine learning and image identification can be used widely in the world, and this is my final intention of doing this programme.
