Data analysis

Pandas

Recently, I have learnt some interesting functions and methods about the module Pandas, which are often used to process tables.

<Image from https://i.ytimg.com/vi/ZxC771f04rQ/maxresdefault.jpg>

I saw an interesting data yesterday which was about the history of the Google stock price.

After downloading the data from the pandas_datareader, I saw the head (first 5 lines) of the stock price from 2004-08-19.

Also, matplotlib can be used to visualize the closed price here. As shown in the graph, although there were some fluctuations around 2008, the overall trend of the stock price is rising.

Then, I have learnt how to resample the data according to new frequency, such as ‘D’ for day, and ‘H’ for hour. Here, the ‘BA’ stands for business year end, so the mean price of each year is calculated, and the value of which is put on the last working day of the year. This is shown by the dotted dashed line. By contrast, asfreq('BA') is used to find the price of the last working day of the year, so the curve is less smooth than the resampled one.

What is more, I also learnt the shift() and tshift() method. shift() method is used to shift the data forward or backward and tshift() is used to shift the DateTimeIndex. Here, in the first graph, it is the original data, and in the 2nd graph, the data shifts to the right by 900 days, so there is NaN data on the left. In the 3rd graph, the DateTimeIndex shifts to the left by 900 days. The red vertical line is drawn to represent the same position on the curve. By the way, loc=2 in the legend method is used to locate the legend in the top left corner, and .set(weight='heavy') is used to make the font bold.

In addition, the shift in time can be applied to see the return of investing.

If the data shifts to the left by 365 days i.e. 1 year, and divides by the original data and minus 1, this will be the percentage change in the value of the stock. For example, if the price in 2006 is $100, and the price in 2007 is $120, the percentage increase in value is 2007/2006-1 .

Finally, I learnt the rolling method of the DataFrame and Series, which was used to achieve the rolling view.

The main idea here, in my opinion, is to choose 365 data, and to set the centre value as the label, meaning that the value of either .mean() or .std() or something else is given to the central value to represent the whole 365 data. This reminds me of the convolution operation which I learnt in the Garbage Classification programme. The rolling view is quite similar to the convolution in the CNN (Convolutional Neural Network). The convolutional kernel here is 365 * 1 matrix, and each 365 data undergoes the convolution.

Just like the graph that I have sketched here, 365 days are a window, and the window roll across the data, which is similar to the idea of convolution.

In conclusion, I have learnt:

  1. DateTimeIndex
  2. Resample & asfreq() methods
  3. ‘ffill()’ & ‘bfill()’ in asqfreq() methods
  4. shift() & tshift() methods
  5. Rolling View (analogous to CNN)
