The biggest failure I experienced

Posted on 2020-06-07 | In Activities & Feelings |

Being a successful student requires a variety of skills including time management and keeping good focus; I learnt this important fact during my experience of failing the ZhongKao exam which at the time, had a very negative impact on me. Ultimately though, it taught me a lot, and offered me insight into what failure can motivate us to achieve although it can be a very difficult learning curve.

<Image from Here>

During the 3 years of studying at Middle High school, I did not study very hard and I clearly remember that in the 2nd year, almost each afternoon, I could be found playing badminton after school.

<Image from Here )

After two academic years, I realized that my mock exam score had declined rapidly due to my focus on other activities, so in the 3rd year, which is the last year of Junior High, I decided to study harder, but it was too late to return to my previous high standards. Eventually, when I received my score for the very important test, I was shocked because due to my poor score, I couldn’t apply to any mainstream schools which was my dream. At that moment, I felt tearful and distraught but on reflection I realised I had to analyse why my performance was so poor and discover my underlying problem.

<Image from Here)

The subsequent soul-searching and quest to take something positive from my experience of failure led me to some important revelations. First of all, think twice before doing anything; If I had thought about the result of playing badminton instead of studying every day, I would have studied much harder. By studying harder for these 3 years, many years later I could then enjoy playing. In addition, due to this poor application to my studies and low motivation, I could have regretted it for many years in the future Therefore, nowadays, before doing anything, I always ask myself three questions: Would you regret it if you do not do it? Is it the best decision to make at the moment given my current circumstances? What are the advantages and disadvantages of not doing it?’. These questions now allow me try to make more rational decisions.

<Image from Here )

Secondly, I have learnt the importance of a character trait called persistence. I have come to realise that every success can rarely exist without persistence, although it can be argued that it is often easier to give up when life becomes challenging and look for a way out. Through my experience of failing and losing motivation, it is clear that temptations such as electronic devices and phone calls cause someone to get distracted and become unfocused, and if this happens, I will think about the difference between who I am currently and who I want to be in the future. Suddenly, I will refocus on the meaningful things, which include long-term goals and my own set of values which include patience and perseverance.

<Image from Here)

This new approach and mindset have resulted in excellent results in Senior High School. For example, after working hard for years, I have become the top student in the monthly test and the end of semester tests, and now I continue to work hard to maintain my focus and determination, hoping to apply to a good university.

<Image from Here )

My final piece of advice is just do it instead of thinking too much. In the past, I sometimes had a plan, but possible imagined difficulties would cause me to give up easily. Now, instead, I just do it, and adjust the plan during the process, which I found is the most efficient way to take me to a successful outcome although it is true decisions have to be made on a case by case basis. For instance, several months ago, I hesitated as to whether I should learn the Python language, because it seemed very complicated and a step too far for me but I decided to try it. Indeed, initially, I could not understand any lines, but I did not give up as I may have done before. Spending more time and energy on it, I have finally learnt some useful skills, and during the process learnt, I began to study the subject area in more depth and I have been deeply involved in a waste disposal programme which I am finding very interesting.

<Image from Here)

In conclusion, after experiencing the biggest failure of my life, I have spent some valuable time finding the reasons for it and applied the solutions to my current studies and life. These realisations and insights discussed above have improved not only my studies but also my life skills, offering me a much more positive and balanced outlook on how failure can be understood as a learning experience for self- improvement and be the springboard for success rather than a reason for self-pity and discouragement.
