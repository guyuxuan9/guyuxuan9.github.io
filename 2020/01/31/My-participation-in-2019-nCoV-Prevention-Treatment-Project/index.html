Extra-curricular activities

My participation in 2019-nCoV Prevention & Treatment Project

Yesterday, on the 30th of January, the World Health Organization has declared that the coronavirus in China a global health emergency.

I was shocked when hearing this news early in the morning. Initially, I thought it was only a regional outbreak in Wuhan, the city at the centre of the outbreak, but then the virus has spread to almost everywhere in China, and now around the world. Until 30th of January, the number of deaths has increased to 170, and there were 7711 confirmed cases and 1370 suspected cases.

First of all, I felt so depressed about this news, and wanted to thank for all the doctors and nurses fighting for the coronavirus, and felt so sorry for those who have suffered from this virus during the Chinese New Year. Then, I decided to do something for patients and medical staffs from all over the world, especially Wuhan, China.
As a citizen, I have been told by the news that the full protecting clothes for medical staffs and facial masks everyone were in short supply but in high demand. In addition, I realised that some materials must be in shortage, because this size of this 2019-nCoV was unprecedented. Therefore, I wanted to use my faint action to help patients who needs the warmth from everyone. As the saying goes, ‘Actions speak louder than words’, I decided to donate my own money from the ‘red packets’ to help people suffering from this disease, especially in Wuhan.
Fortunately, I found the ‘Novel Coronavirus Prevention & Treatment Projects’ and donated 500 RMB to the Project Hope, a charity organisation. Although, the money itself has no intrinsic value, the compassion and kindness behind this activity is invaluable, which cannot be measured using money.
Furthermore, I wanted to say thank you to foreign people who hold great concern about the fighting for virus in China. It was your greeting and encouragement that gave us confidence and strength. The virus does not need a passport, and we are the community of shared future for mankind. Virus can be isolated, but the compassion and kindness cannot. The compassion and kindness are like a bridge, connecting people from all over the world.

Finally, I hope that with appropriate prevention and confidence, which can be seen as the best vaccine, we can definitely win the smokeless battle!
