Feelings after getting the award

Today, finally, I got the silver award for the 2019 BPho.

Looking at the certificate, I had many words to say. Although it is only a silver award, it is quite unique for me. I have known this competition since the summer holiday in 2019. Having a first look at the past paper of BPho, I nearly can’t do any of those questions, because I only knew a little from my IGCSE course, which was absolutely not enough. Then I decided to try my best to work out as many questions as possible. It was really a tough time for me, especially at the beginning. I went to school at daytime, so the only time for me was the evening. Every day, I felt so exhausted after a busy school day, but for the BPho, for my dream, I kept telling myself ‘Come on !’. A few weeks later, I managed to do some of the questions, and I felt a little proud for myself.

On 16th of November, 2019, I attended the BPho. After getting the paper, I focused on my paper and did not think about anything else. As time passed by, the questions became more difficult, and at some instant, I was a little bit impatient, because there were more and more questions that I can’t manage to do, but later on, I told myself ‘Calm down! Try your best, and it will be fine’. Therefore, one question successfully reminded me of a question about the spring oscillator, which I have done before, which inspired me a lot. I still remembered that question, which was about letting a double planks connected by two strings falling from a height, and the two strings will make the planks to do simple harmonic motion. This gave me a lot of confidence, and encouraged me to do more questions. Although it was difficult to pay 100% attention throughout the 160 minutes, and there were some questions I did wrong which I should have done it correctly, I was appreciated when the competition has finished, because I applied at least 70% of what I have learnt. Meanwhile, I felt a little bit upset, because I thought there would be some questions about the electric circuits in section 2, such as the Kirchhoff’s law and the Wheatstone bridge, but they did not appear in the paper.

Now, I got the result. I was fortunate, and I want to say thank you to my father, who helped me a lot during the preparation of the BPho, and of course, I want to say thank you to myself. It is me who kept challenging the extreme and never gave up.

This silver award is just a starting point. There is still a long way to go. Well done and keep it up!
